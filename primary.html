The primary goal of databases

The primary goal of databases is to focus on the way data is organized, not on specific applications. That is, the main goal of the database designer is to design the data so that it is free from repetition and can be retrieved, modified and added to it without the problems that can occur with the presence of repetition in it. This is done by creating three levels of abstraction or models for databases called normalization models (database normalization), and they are intended to make the data structure closer to the taxonomic nature.
